GDD Lab and Studio Rules

  1. Students are expected to abide by the following GDD lab and studio rules:
    • Students may only access GDD labs and studios using their UFV Student ID card.
    • The doors to the GDD labs and studios must remain closed and may not be propped open.
    • The last student to leave D244 or D227 must ensure doors to exterior patios are locked.
    • No guests â€” including family members are permitted in the GDD labs or studios.
  2. D244: Thunderbolt workstations are for GDD diploma students. iMac workstations are for other students taking GD courses. As a GD student, you will be assigned a workstation for each course. Students are not permitted to access the instructor workstation.
  3. Unauthorized use of fonts, software, network fi les or electronic files, and the use of unlicensed software will not be tolerated.
  4. MacBook Pro users are responsible for the proper maintenance of their own computer and equipment, and are responsible for reading licenses and warranty information supplied with each piece of equipment.
  5. Changes to the computer network or power wiring is NOT permitted.6. Students are responsible for backing up their fi les to their own personal storage device. The fi le server is for course work only and may NOT be used for back up.
  6. UFV is not responsible for the loss of any files left on any server or system.
  7. Peer to peer sharing from off-campus websites, such as bittorents and hack sites, is strictly prohibited.
  8. Personal mobile devices may not be used during class time.10. Use of the internet, social media and e-mail during class time is limited to course work.11. Students must report any malfunctioning GDD equipment (including iMacs, Thunderbolt displays, scanners, or printers) the GDD lab technicians via email gddlabtech@ufv.ca.
  9. Noise level must be kept to a minimum. No music or video is permitted in the classroom except for that played through a personal headset at the discretion of the instructor.13. Food/drinks are not allowed at any computer stations.
  10. Wet media are not allowed in the Mac labs or photo studios. The use of spray materials (glues, paints, etc.) and solvents other than Gamsol and Galkyd is not permitted in any indoor space in any building at UFV.
  11. Students are responsible for the security of their own software, personal equipment, materials and supplies. Lockers are assigned to diploma students at the beginning of first year.
  12. UFV is not responsible for lost or stolen items and equipment. Personal computer peripherals (mouse, keyboard, tablet) may be left at assigned workstations at your own risk.
  13. Clean computer workstations at the end of each use, Please make use of the trash and recycling bins. 18. Do not mark or cut on desktops. Cutting is only permitted on the cutting mats. Steel rulers and other tools provided in production studios must remain in each respective lab.

  15. Students taking GD courses on the Mission campus will be assigned a temporary lab access card. Lost lab access cards must be reported to the GDDDepartment Assistant . Access cards that are not returned at the end of course completion are subject to $25 replacement fee.
