GDD Software Guide

Here you will find instruction for the installation and use of the software you will use. As of right now it is solely software available for remote students.

Adobe#

At the beginning of each semester we add you our back-end console which gives you licenses to the entire Creative Cloud list of apps.

Creative Cloud Install – MAC#

  1. Once you are added to the back-end you will recieve an email letting you know you have access. If you do not recieve this email check your spam folder or try to continue on to the next step.
  2. Go to the Adobe Account Page and log in or create an account using your student email firstname.lastname@student.ufv.ca.
important

Do not log in with the "Continue with Google" "Continue with Facebook" or "Continue with Apple" buttons. Do not log in with a personal account you may have already used in the past or you will not have access to the apps that we license for you. If you are logged in, please log out and continue to log in with your student email.

  1. When you are logged in successfully, proceed to Access your apps and services from here you can simply press install on any of the apps your instructors have informed you to use.
  2. We do however recommend that you start by installing creative cloud desktop application from here just follow any of the onscreen instructions for installation.

Universal Type Client#

GDD uses the Extensis Universal Type Server (UTS) and Universal Type Client (UTC) 7 to keep your system stable through the activation and deactivation of fonts and font management. Through our font server, you have access to the entire Adobe Font Folio of more than 2,500 type faces.

Install UTC#

Click and download the appropriate installer MAC or PC

Follow the onscreen installation instructions and restart your computer.

How to activate UTC#

note

Please note the server address shown in this video is currently out of date please use 198.162.98.197

Make sure that your software is up to date and logged in:

  1. Launch the Universal Type Client application.
  2. If this is your first time opening UTC, the program will display a connect-to-server dialogue.
  3. Enter the IP address 198.162.98.197 to connect to the server. Do not use the “browse servers” option.
  4. Enter 8080 as the port number.
  5. User Name: students and Password: get the password from your instructor or lab tech
  6. Click connect.

How to use UTC#

FONT ACTIVATION

  • Temporary 🔵: activated fonts and sets turn off once your Mac is shut down
  • Permanent 🟢: fonts will remain activated until turned off by the user
