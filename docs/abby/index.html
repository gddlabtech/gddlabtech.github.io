Abbotsford Studio Guide

K160â€‹

K160 is the temporary Pod Classroom where the GDD MacBooks will be used. It is equipped with an Apple TV for screen mirroring to the projector, as well as 6 Large Screens for each of the pods. The Xerox Versalink printer will be located in this room as well.

Projector and Podsâ€‹

The podium can broadcast to the 4 screens and projector simultaneously, or the 4 pods can connect individually to their particular screen. Under each TV are the wall plates that allow you to turn On and Off the TV. They also allow you to switch input from the HDMI port at each pod or from the instructor's podium. There are also wall power outlets for your laptop; additional chargers and power bars are located in the storage above the laptop cart. Here you will also find the HDMI cords that you will need to plug in your laptop via USB-C to the wall port at each pod.

In addition to the pods, we also have a brand-new projector system in here that can be controlled or take the video input from the instructor station up at the front with the podium, or from the Apple TV in the podium itself now the system that controls this is to be used only by faculty and instructors arm and if you are a student that is going to be presenting from up here you will need to have the instructor set it up for you first. That being said, to get the projector up and to work, whether you want to output to all the screens in the room or just to the projector, you can choose which one you want to do here. When you walk up to the Extron display on the podium, there are two buttons to turn on the system one says "present using projector only." The other one says, "present using projector and pod displays." You have four display input options once you decide which display output you wish to use from the podium. One of them is PC output. There is a Windows PC computer built into the podium if there are no other computers available. Next, there is HDMI one, which is for a laptop with just HDMI; there's USB-C for MacBooks, or you can choose to output from the Apple TV.

If you are a student sitting at a pod and want to show something on your screen to all of your fellow classmates. Get the instructor to select Apple TV as the video output to display on all screens. From your own MacBook, choose from the screen mirroring options to airplay to C1420 Apple TV. This will allow you to share your screen on all the screens in the room and the projector. If you are just wanting to share your screen on your own individual pod, this is where are you where you would use the HDMI port with the cables that are stored in the cabinet.

K160 Laptopsâ€‹

You are welcome to use your own laptop, but if you do not have access to one, we have 20 available for your use. These are all stored in the cart at the back of the classroom. At the start of each semester, we will assign you your own specific laptop; you will be able to find this list around the room.

The laptop that you are assigned is your responsibility. You are personally responsible for getting it plugged in at the end of your course and being sanitized and put away. Note that none of these laptops may leave K160 for any reason! Even if you were printing to the Konica in Building C, you must print from K160 and then walk back to get your parts must not bring the laptop with you. This is very important.

If you do not have a laptop assigned, please wait until everyone else takes a computer and then take one that's not in use, but please let the GDD technicians know which one you took.

At the end of each class, please make sure that you plug the laptop back into the cart. These laptops in the cart have a specific orientation to all be plugged in, so please note the sticker on top of the computer as to which way the laptop needs to face out to plug into the charger.

K162 Mac Studio Labâ€‹

K162 is the main GDD Mac lab. It is equipped with 25 brand new Mac Studios and Thunderbolt displays for your MacBooks. Here is where the majority of your lab classes will be held.

C1120 Mac Labâ€‹

C1120 is the main MacLab for the School of Creative Arts. This room has 25 Mac Studios, plus an instructor Mac Studio, and an Extron projector.

Drop in times are posted on the MacLab door. Please ask your instructor for the door codes.

C1122 Homework Studioâ€‹

Room C1122 is the smaller of the 2 Maclabs on the Abbotsford Campus.

It consists of Mac Studios installed with Adobe CC, and UTC. you will also have access to a black and white printer, and a photo scanner.

Please ask your instructor for the door code.

note

The Mac Labs will be available for drop in use from 7:00 AM - 10:00 PM Weekdays during times between classes in C1120, and 8:00 AM - 6:00 PM on Saturdays. The class schedule is posted on the door of C1120. The Mac Labs are closed on Sundays.

important

No food or drink is allowed in the MacLabs
